From 150 to 67:WR to reduce 
 cancellation of locals from Oct 2.






Mumbai: After deciding to cancel 150 local services from Monday till Oct 4 
due to a 30kmph speed restriction being imposed on all four lines between Ram Mandir and Malad stations 
owing to ongoing work on the fifth and sixth lines,Western Railways(WR) has slashed the number of cancellations
to 67 from Wednesday. Besides, it said,normal operations will be completely restored on Friday.


  Vineet Abhishek, WR's chief public relations officer said,"The speed restriction on the fast corridor will be
lifted by Wednesday morning, resulting in a decrease in the number of cancellations of suburban train services 
from around 150 to 67."The speed restriction on the slow lines will be removed by Friday morning and suburban 
servies will resume normal operations from Friday, with no cancellations during the day, expcet during block 
periods, he said.


  Abhishek said construction work on the sixth line between Goregaon and Kandivali will be finished by this 
weekend when the last and final major block for the project will be undertaken. He said all efforts are being 
made to ensure completion of the work commencement od Navaratri Festival.The fifth and sixth lines are a crit-
ical infrastructure project aimed at easing congestion on Mumbai's crowded suburban network.





BMC & Rlys to up drainage 
 capacity to crub flooding.






Mumbai: Following severe waterlogging on railway tracks at seven location after the 
heavy rain of Sept 25 evening in just five hours, a meeting was convenced on Monday between BMC and railway 
officals. The waterlogging had caused disruption in railway services, leaving commuters stranded for hours.
The city had received 200 mm rain in several parts of the eastern suburbs, including CSMT yard, Bhandup, 
Vikhroli, Kurla, Vidyavihar, Sewri and Mankhurd.


   During the meeting officials reached a consensus on several critical measures. It was decided to undertake
works aimed at widening and enhancing drainage capacity in these areas. In addition to improving existing 
infrastructure, discussions also focused on the removal of nearby slums that have been obstructing the drainage
systems.


Additional municipal commissioner Abhijit Bangar said, "Efforts should be made to complete the works as quickly
before the next monsoon. Decisions on whether to use traditional methods or micro-tunnelling for expansion of 
culverts under railway tracks will be taken based on the specific locations."
